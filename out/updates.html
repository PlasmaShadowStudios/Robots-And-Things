Updates And Developer Map

Completed Milestones

v0.4

Base Gameplay

The original 2021 demo with all the base moves, levels, and collectables.

v0.5

More Content

Major un-lockable abilities, more levels, worlds, and bosses. A complete first draft of the game.

v0.6

Move Meter

Better feedback for what moves you can use in the air, plus rework how many charges you have per move.

v0.7

Score Elements

Chain moves off enemies to score points. Use your points across levels to unlock boss levels and progress through the game.

2023-12-15


https://youtu.be/ZuJUEl0u6fY Here's a quick update now with some short video clips. First this is a more refined version of the move meter that was prototyped in the last update. A spin jump takes two charges and a dive takes one, out of the three charges you start with. It can be upgraded to have up to 6 charges as you progress in order to spin and dive extra times, in addition to performing more powerful and more costly abilities.




https://youtu.be/0XfvFZ90Tkw Aaaand here's a score system I've been experimenting with. You can get high scores in levels now that emphasizes chaining moves in the air. As you chain moves you increase a multiplier that gives you more points as you bounce off enemies. Major collectables also would give you a ton of points as well. All your high scores combined between levels (and from collectibles in hub worlds) could potentially be used as a way to progress through the game.



Other than that there might be a few other changes, like challenge medals I've been reworking to be needed to unlock a single challenge level in each world, and said level would reward you with more gears than usual or the strong power ups like the rocket. As for general development no the game's not totally dead. ðŸ˜› It's still being worked on bit by bit. I do want to get a "world 1" kind of demo out that has tweaked levels with these two new systems up at some point. Not sure when but most of the pieces of the world are already in place for that.

2023-02-02


https://giphy.com/gifs/1mkNO6EFohivp9LOLj Tiny update: **The Move Meter** Check out the top right of that gif to see a proof of concept meter that adds visual feedback to move chaining. Each move costs "charges" to execute. A spin jump costs 4, the dive costs 1, and a kick costs 2. Staying on the ground or bouncing off stuff can refill the meter. (Also the kick got an experimental revamp that makes it more like an air dash that halts all gravity. It's like a dive that you can aim but slightly more costly.)




https://giphy.com/gifs/mTmms2xesjpIVOwzWK It's mostly the same feel as chaining moves before, but with better feedback to know what you can do midair. Plus if the move meter could increase permanently or temporarily, that could allow for a lot more crazy move chaining.
